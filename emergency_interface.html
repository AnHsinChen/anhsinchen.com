The Challenges

Parents with minimal medical training face life-or-death decisions in moments of panic.

When a child's heart pump fails, parents have only 2 minutes to replace the controller before loss of consciousness. The current interface displays cryptic alerts like "Change Controller" with no visual guidance, expecting caregivers to remember complex procedures from a 300-page manual during extreme stress.

Formative Analysis

After talking with experts and digging through documents, I found that users have three main goals, and each one has some gaps we need to fill. Users interact across multiple touchpoints - both digital and physical interfaces - which creates a complex user journey. To scope the project and establish technical requirements, I created an Effort-Impact matrix to prioritize our initiatives.

  • Goal 1: Device Management & Monitoring
  • Goal 2: Emergency Response
  • Goal 3: Daily Living Integration

From analysis, I prioritized concepts that delivered the highest impact with relatively low effort. Among these, critical alert redesign and alarm context information stood out as essential because they directly affect user safety under stress. Once the direction was set, we explored how medical device could assist users during these critical moments.

Evaluation

I conducted 30-minute one-on-one sessions with 8 participants. Each session was divided into three phases: a background interview, an direct observation, and a post-interview including walkthrough using think-aloud protocol. During the observation phase, participants were given a scenario and asked to follow the animation flow to complete the Change Controller procedure. All sessions were video- and audio-recorded for note-taking purposes.

Scenario

The top 3 pain points evident from the data were

  • Instruction text misinterpreted: Multiple participants misinterpreted text in several steps (S0, S2, S4, S5, S6). Especially clustered confusion around steps 2, 4, 5, and 6.

The Solution

This design helps users quickly recognize critical alerts, interpret instructions correctly, and take the right actions at the right time. By streamlining both visual and textual cues, the system reduces errors and improves confidence during high-stress scenarios.

Being clear in critical moments

Creating step-by-step guidance with numbered markers and added detail so that users donâ€™t miss actions, even under stress. This clarity reduces mistakes and builds confidence during emergencies.

Being intuitive through visuals

Using colors, icons, and emotional feedback (heart states) to instantly communicate broken vs. new controllers and safe vs. unsafe conditions. This lowers cognitive load and speeds up recognition.

Being supportive with emergency-optimized language

Crafting short, directive, and reassuring instructions designed for high-pressure situations. This language style helps users act quickly without confusion or hesitation.
