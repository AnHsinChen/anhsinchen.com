AImpower Zoom Plugin

A Zoom plugin that makes virtual meetings less stressful for people who stutter.

Read Blog

OVERVIEW

The AImpower.org is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit committed to amplify marginalized voices in the investigation and development of emerging technologies.

The aim of this project was to bridge the communication gap for people who stutter in video conferencing. As one of three UX designers on the project team, I collaborated with my design partners to transform research insights into interactive prototypes, enabling our research team to validate concepts with users and ultimately delivering high-fidelity designs ready for engineering handoff.

ROLE & DURATION

UX Designer

The Challenges

People who stutter face significant challenges in video meetings that are often invisible to other participants.

Video calls have become the dominant way we communicate at work, but current platforms weren't designed with speech diversity in mind. Features like self-view and voice-controlled turn-taking can actually make stuttering worse, while the lack of nonverbal cues makes it harder to connect with others.

Without accessible communication tools, people who stutter experience increased anxiety, physical tension, and emotional exhaustion during video calls. They often engage in avoidance behaviors and require substantial invisible effort just to participate effectively. This creates missed opportunities to fully contribute their ideas and expertise in virtual meetings.

Research Synthesis

AImpower's research team had identified pain points across the three phases

  • BEFORE videoconferences: Preparation involves goal setting, planning self-disclosure strategies, customizing the physical environment, and engaging in mindfulness practices to reduce pre-meeting anxiety.
  • DURING videoconferences: Real-time support includes emotional affirmations, audience connection tools, turn-taking assistance, mindfulness cues, and vulnerability support features that help people who stutter participate more authentically.
  • AFTER videoconferences: Post-meeting activities focus on reflection and journaling for emotional processing, goal assessment, and building community connections with supportive participants.

We synthesized the findings to develop journey maps and personas.

Kicking off the Design

With the insights in hand, we began brainstorming and used affinity diagramming to cluster design ideas. We narrowed down to 6 design directions and gathered feedback by presenting them to stakeholders and the research team. After receiving approval from our project manager, we started creating low-fidelity prototypes.

Fig: Feedback on 6 selected design directions

Understand Zoom Ecosystem

After finalizing our feature set, we focused on understanding the Zoom ecosystem and its technical constraints. We explored competitor plugins on the Zoom App Marketplace to analyze their design patterns, UI layouts, and interaction models. Additionally, we collaborated with the AImpower engineering team to identify technical limitations early in the process. Once we validated our user flows and low-fidelity concepts, we moved forward with high-fidelity prototyping.

Fig: Low-fi Prototype

The Solution

The solution enables people who stutter to navigate video meetings with greater confidence through personalized preparation tools, real-time support features, and post-meeting reflection capabilities.
By addressing both emotional and technical barriers, the design reduces communication stress and enhances meaningful participation for users while fostering understanding among other attendees.

Raise Hand Function

Offers a visual hand-raising feature to communicate speaking intentions without relying solely on voice.

Hold Speech Turn

Uses calming visual cues to facilitate smoother turn-taking and reduce speaking pressure.

Affirmative Messages

Provides customizable positive reinforcement messages to support users during challenging speaking moments.

Name Tag

Enables subtle self-identification through customizable name tags that include pronouns and stuttering disclosure.

Disclosure Message

Provides optional pre-meeting messages that educate participants about stuttering and communication needs.
