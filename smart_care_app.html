The Challenges

Families face a daunting transition from hospital to home care, armed only with a 300-page manual and 120 days of training.

Going home means parents are suddenly on their own with this life-sustaining device. They're trying to juggle dozens of daily care tasks while constantly worrying "Am I doing this right?" Finding answers means flipping through pages and pages of technical documentation when they just need to know one simple thing.

The mental exhaustion is real - parents are carrying around this enormous checklist in their heads, trying to remember every step, every warning sign, every procedure, all while dealing with the emotional weight of their child's condition. As one parent put it, "Even putting him to bed, I'm lying there wondering if I missed something important.

Research Synthesis

After talking with experts and digging through documents, I found that users have three main goals, and each one has some gaps we need to fill. Users interact across multiple touchpoints - both digital and physical interfaces - which creates a complex user journey. To scope the project and establish technical requirements, I created an Effort-Impact matrix to prioritize our initiatives.

For this case study, I narrowed down the project scope to focus specifically on how a mobile solution could fit into families' everyday routines, since there isn't one out there yet. I picked some of the gaps and defined how an app could contribute.

  • Reminder System: The app could send notifications for exit site care and equipment cleaning schedules with visual guides to ensure proper sterile technique and safety protocols.
  • Emergency Contact Quick Access: A one-touch emergency button could instantly connect users to their hospital contact with automatic location sharing, while displaying critical device information and patient ID details.
  • Simplified Status Dashboard: The app could translate complex system controller data into intuitive visual summaries, showing battery life, pump status, and system health.
  • Daily Routine Guidance: The app could provide contextual checklists and visual guides for different daily activities like showering, sleeping, and traveling.
  • Alarm Context Information: When system alarms occur, the app could provide plain-language explanations of what each alarm means and clear guidance on when to call hospital contacts versus handling issues independently.
